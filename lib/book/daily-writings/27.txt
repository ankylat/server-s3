Ser libre consiste en ejercer,  la ausencia de miedo, en operar espontaneamente, sin un apego que te ate a una idea en particular, o si alguna idea te ata, pues ejercer la libertad de decir que no, por ejemplo.

***

everything was just out of reach and I can still recall that frustration boiling over me. I would slither, bounce, and sway toward the object of my desire. I was never satisfied. My temper would burst into flames anytime I saw how easily others pranced or strolled about the world, plucking from the world what it provided. Then one day, my legs felt this funny feeling like they were telling me something about myself. They were telling me to stretch them out and wobble, and fall, and do whatever I could do escape my miserable stagnant existence. The process was painful at times. I recall wanting to give up, briefly, but the mad desire and feeling returned to my legs. My default was to stand on them both and to move forward. Today I almost do not believe that I was ever once stuck that way. I take for granted that I can move about, gracefully or clumsily, and I too can catch my heart's dream or fill my tummy with what I want to take. My existence before I learned to walk... It's almost too painful to recall. I have dreams now that I never used to have, like jumping and soaring through the air. I feel the wind on my face as I run toward my destiny. I live each day with purpose. I am reminded of how fortunate I am, and that some day, I may lose my skills as I age, and I may be ridden to a chair or walker. I try to live with more purpose now, since I can walk. 

***

Learning how to walk introduced additional mobility into my life: I could get to places faster and go much further.  Another change was it raised my perspective from my hands and knees to being on two feet.  

This new perspective illuminated new ideas and previously unseen objects that were always there.  

It allowed me to progress to more challenges, like running, jumping, reaching for the sky.

I was able to be more independent and go places without having to be carried, but could rather walk alongside of.

I could start to carry things or pull them.  

It opened up access to things like playing on jungle gyms, playing games with other people, and even just standing in one spot.
